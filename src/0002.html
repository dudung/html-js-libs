# view $\LaTeX$ equations in HTML using MathJax
## result
A quadratic equation $$\tag{1}\label{eq1} y = ax^2 + bx + c, $$ will have two roots of $x_1$ and $x_2$, which are given by $$\tag{2}\label{eq2} x_{1,2} = \frac{-b \mp \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}, $$ that is known as quadratic formula. Substitusion of $x_1$ and $x_2$ from Equation $\eqref{eq2}$ to $\eqref{eq1}$ will make $y = 0$.
## code Previous result is obtained using
```html A quadratic equation $$\tag{1}\label{eq1} y = ax^2 + bx + c, $$ will have two roots of $x_1$ and $x_2$, which are given by $$\tag{2}\label{eq2} x_{1,2} = \frac{-b \mp \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}, $$ that is known as quadratic formula. Substitusion of $x_1$ and $x_2$ from Equation $\eqref{eq2}$ to $\eqref{eq1}$ will make $y = 0$. ```
in body section and with
```js <script type="text/javascript" async src="https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/mathjax/2.7.7/latest.js?config=TeX-MML-AM_CHTML"> </script> ```
in head section of a HTML file.
